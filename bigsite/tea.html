Tea

Tea is an aromatic beverage made by steeping the cured leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant in hot water. Major varieties include green tea, black tea, oolong, and white tea, which differ mainly in how much the leaves are oxidised.

Green tea is only lightly processed and keeps a grassy flavour, while black tea is fully oxidised and stronger. Herbal infusions such as chamomile and peppermint are often called tea although they contain no Camellia leaves.

Like coffee, tea contains caffeine, but usually in smaller amounts per cup.
