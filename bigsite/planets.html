Planets

The solar system contains eight planets orbiting the Sun. In order from the Sun they are Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

Jupiter is the largest planet, a gas giant with a famous great red spot storm. Mars, the red planet, is a target for robotic exploration and future missions. Each planet follows an elliptical orbit governed by gravity.

Earth is the only planet known to support life, with vast oceans of liquid water covering most of its surface.
