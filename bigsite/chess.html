Chess

Chess is a two player strategy board game played on a square board of sixty four squares arranged in an eight by eight grid. Each player begins with sixteen pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two bishops, two knights, and eight pawns.

The goal is to checkmate the opponent king, meaning the king is under attack and cannot escape. Strong play involves controlling the centre, developing pieces in the opening, and calculating tactics such as forks and pins.

Modern chess engines use search algorithms and evaluation functions to choose strong moves, which connects the game to computer science.
