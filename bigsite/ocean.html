Ocean

The ocean is the body of salt water that covers more than seventy percent of the surface of the Earth. It is divided into five major regions: the Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, and Arctic oceans.

Ocean waves are driven by the wind, while tides are caused by the gravity of the Moon and Sun. The deep sea is home to marine life including fish, whales, and vast coral reefs that shelter thousands of species.

Oceans play a major role in climate by storing heat and carbon dioxide.
